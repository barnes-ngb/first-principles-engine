## 1) Family Snapshot

- Parents: Shelly (fibromyalgia; loves Bible study; AA in theology) + Nathan (R&D engineer; technology/programming/discovery).
- Kids: Lincoln (10) — speech + neurodivergence; ~3rd grade math; ~1st grade reading; phonics recently clicking. London (6) — kindergarten; story-driven; creates own books.
- Time windows: Shelly leads a 2-3 hour block most weekdays (split between kids); Nathan leads Saturday 10am-1pm + occasional evenings.
- Current curriculum/tools: The Good and the Beautiful (Lincoln), Reading Eggs (nearing completion for Lincoln), parent-led read-alouds, Dad Saturday Build Lab.

## 2) North Star

We are forming whole boys who know they are loved by God, grow in character, master core skills, and develop courage and competence through adventure, work, and discovery.

Three integrated tracks:
- **Formation** (spiritual + character): identity in Christ, virtue practice, prayer, Scripture, relational health.
- **Mastery** (core academics): reading, writing, math — measurable progress without shame.
- **Adventure** (body + curiosity): movement, nature, making/building, field trips, camping, service.

## 3) Energy Modes (Two Tiers)

Aligned with Charter v1.0. Two modes, not three:

### Normal Day (full routine)
Formation anchor + Reading stations + Math stations + Together Block. This is the target on good-energy days.

### Minimum Viable Day (the floor)
When energy is low, we protect the essentials and let the rest go without guilt:
- Prayer + Scripture + gratitude (short, steady)
- Read aloud (shared story time counts)
- Math practice (short and consistent)
- Project or life-skills block (hands-on wins)
- One-sentence reflection (photo + "what I learned")

**Both modes count as real school.** The app tracks energy state daily and adjusts the suggested plan accordingly. There is no "Plan B" — just Normal and MVD. This language is consistent across the charter, the engine, and the app.

### Energy State in the App
- Shelly selects energy level at the start of each day (or the app prompts based on patterns)
- Normal Day loads full block schedule
- MVD loads the five essentials only
- The adaptive loop tracks energy patterns over time and suggests proactive adjustments (e.g., "Tuesdays have been low-energy 3 of the last 4 weeks — consider making Tuesday a standing MVD")

## 4) Weekly Rhythm

### Weekdays (Shelly, split-block model)

Shelly's attention is the primary schedulable resource. The split-block model:

**Block 1 — Lincoln direct (Shelly focused on Lincoln, London independent):**
- Reading stations: phonics, decodable reading, sight words, spelling
- Math: TGTB lesson or practice

**Block 2 — London direct (Shelly focused on London, Lincoln independent):**
- Early literacy: phonemic awareness, phonics, handwriting
- Read-aloud + narration
- Book-making / creative activity

**Together Block (both kids, lower Shelly energy required):**
- Formation anchor: prayer + short Bible reading + heart question
- Rotate: science / read-aloud + narration / nature journal / art
- Movement or outdoor time

**Note:** The exact order and duration varies by day and energy. The app generates a suggested schedule based on energy state, pace data, and split-block constraint.

### Saturday (Nathan, 10am-1pm)
- 10:00-10:10 — Mission + prayer (set tone)
- 10:10-11:20 — Build Lab (engineering/science build tied to weekly theme)
- 11:20-11:45 — Notebook time (draw/write: what happened, what changed, what learned)
- 11:45-12:30 — Iterate / test / improve
- 12:30-1:00 — Outside movement (walk, sport drills, hike)

## 5) Shared Theme Engine

One theme every 1-2 weeks (e.g., Forces & Motion, Weather, Plants, Maps). Both boys study the same theme; outputs scale by age.

Example outputs:
- London (K): draw observations + 3 vocabulary words + simple tell-back
- Lincoln (10): measure/record data, short explanation, and one "why" question
- Dad Lab: build something that demonstrates the theme

## 6) Subject Mapping to Missouri Requirements

All five MO core subjects are covered:

| MO Required Subject | Engine Coverage | Notes |
|---------------------|----------------|-------|
| Reading | Lincoln: TGTB + Reading Eggs + phonics block. London: read-alouds + early phonics + book-making | Primary focus for both |
| Language Arts | Lincoln: TGTB spelling/grammar. London: handwriting, storytelling, narration | Integrated with reading |
| Mathematics | Lincoln: TGTB (3rd grade). London: K-level counting, number sense, basic operations | Dedicated math block daily |
| Social Studies | Formation track (character, citizenship, community service) + read-alouds (historical/biographical) + theme engine (geography, maps, community) | Tag these activities as "Social Studies" in the app |
| Science | Saturday Build Lab + Theme Engine + weekday Together Block rotations (Mystery Science, TGTB Science, nature journal) | Well covered through multiple channels |

**App implementation:** Activities tagged with `subjectBucket: 'social-studies'` count toward MO core hours. The formation anchor, read-alouds with historical/civic content, and theme weeks covering geography or community topics all qualify.

## 7) Lincoln Focus Areas

Primary bottleneck: foundational reading (phonics + decoding).

Reading block recipe (45 min total, adjustable):
- 10 min — phonemic awareness (blending/segmenting)
- 15-20 min — explicit, systematic phonics lesson
- 10 min — decodable reading (confidence + accuracy)
- 5 min — spelling/dictation (sound-to-symbol mapping)

Keep daily read-alouds for vocabulary/comprehension without pressure.

**Speech:** Was in therapy, not currently active. Clarify targets with Shelly. If active targets exist, integrate 5-10 min/day of speech practice into phonics block where possible.

**Startup friction:** Lincoln struggles to get rolling. The app should offer a low-friction, familiar opener (e.g., a 2-minute Minecraft reading activity or a visual checklist he can start checking off immediately).

## 8) London Focus Areas

Early literacy foundations (short + playful):
- 5-10 min — phonemic awareness games (rhyming, first/last sound, blending)
- 10-15 min — phonics (letter-sound + CVC words)
- 5 min — handwriting (formation + name + simple words)

London's superpower is story. Lean into book-making, illustrated journals, and narration as the primary literacy vehicle. His self-initiated book creation is the highest-leverage activity for emerging literacy.

**Attention needs:** London must feel engaged and attended to. Independent work time ceiling is low (needs investigation). Activities should be interactive, verbal, or creative — never passive worksheets.

## 9) Heart Journey

- Weekly: Dad walk-and-talk with each boy (15-20 min). Prompts: Where were you brave? Where were you afraid? What do you want to learn?
- Monthly: excursion/adventure day (hike, museum, maker space, service project)
- Quarterly: camping/overnight rite (fire, tools, responsibility, stories, gratitude)

## 10) Built-in Supports (Neurodivergence + Speech)

- Short, predictable routines (time blocks) + frequent wins
- Visual checklists and "next step" cues
- Small doses, daily repetition; avoid long frustrating sessions
- Speech practice 5-10 min/day (when targets are clarified); integrate target sounds into phonics and reading when possible
- StartAnyway protocol in the app for refusal/resistance moments
